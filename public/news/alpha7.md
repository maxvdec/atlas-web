## Photon Global Illumination

We added support for Dynamic Diffuse Global Illumination with our new system called Photon. This is a huge step forward for lighting in Atlas Engine, and it will allow you to create much more realistic and immersive lighting in your games. With Photon, you can now have dynamic global illumination that reacts to changes in the scene, which will make your games look much more realistic and visually stunning. We are very excited about the possibilities that Photon opens up for game developers, and we can't wait to see what you create with it!

This basically means that now our renderer takes in account the light that bounces around the scene, which will make the lighting much more realistic and immersive. This is a huge improvement over traditional lighting techniques, which only take into account direct lighting from light sources. With Photon, you can now have dynamic global illumination that reacts to changes in the scene, which will make your games look much more realistic and visually stunning. 

## Photon Path Tracing

We also released the newest addition to our rendering pipeline: Photon Path Tracing. This is a new ray tracing system that will allow us to have much more realistic reflections and shadows in our games. With Photon Path Tracing, you can now have accurate reflections and shadows that react to changes in the scene, which will make your games look much more realistic and visually stunning. We are very excited about the possibilities that Photon Path Tracing opens up for game developers, and we can't wait to see what you create with it!

We rendered a Cornell Box with Photon Path Tracing, and the results are amazing! The reflections and shadows are much more accurate and realistic than with traditional rendering techniques, which will make your games look much more visually stunning. This is a huge improvement over traditional rendering techniques, which only take into account direct lighting from light sources. With Photon Path Tracing, you can now have accurate reflections and shadows that react to changes in the scene, although since it's path tracing, it cannot be used in real-time, but it can be used for baking lightmaps and reflection probes, which will make your games look much more realistic and visually stunning.
