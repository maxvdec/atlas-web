# Moving to Neutral Software

Dear developers,

Since I started working on Atlas Engine, I have been hosting it on my personal GitHub account, which has been a great way to share the project with the world and get feedback from you. However, I think it's time to move on. This is why I have created Neutral Software, a company that basically exists to host Atlas Engine and other projects we might make in the future. This will allow us to have a more professional and organized presence for Atlas Engine, and it will also allow us to have a better way to manage the project and its development.

The rebranding will happen in the next few weeks. All the repositories will be moved to the [Neutral Software organization](https://github.com/neutralsoftware).

This is a big step for us, and we are very excited about the possibilities that this move opens up for us. We are committed to making Atlas Engine the best game engine it can be, and we believe that this move will help us achieve that goal. We are also committed to being transparent and open with our community, and we will keep you updated on the progress of the move and any changes that might happen as a result of it. We want to thank you for your support and feedback throughout the development of Atlas Engine, and we look forward to continuing to work with you and build an amazing game engine together!

Also to celebrate Alpha 9, a huge release, we will have a full rebranding of the website and a new website for Neutral Software. We are very excited about the new website and the new branding, and we can't wait to share it with you. We will keep you updated on the progress of the new website and the new branding, and we will let you know when it's ready to be launched. We hope you will like it as much as we do!

We hope you will continue to support us and Atlas Engine as we move to Neutral Software, and we can't wait to see what you create with Atlas Engine in the future!

Best regards,
Max Van den Eynde, Director of Neutral Software
